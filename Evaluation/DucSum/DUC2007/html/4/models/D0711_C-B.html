[711] In April 2000 Judge Jackson issued his "finding in law" that Microsoft violated the Sherman Antitrust Act, "through predatory and anticompetitive behavior", keeping "an oppressive thumb on the scale of competitive fortune". [711] Microsoft said it would appeal. [711] Microsoft's aggressive marketing practices promoting its browser were judged not illegal. [711] In the June 2000 penalty phase Judge Jackson ordered Microsoft be split into two separate companies: one to handle operating systems and one to handle other products, because "Microsoft has proved untrustworthy", citing Microsoft's failure to comply with a separate 1990's court ruling. [711] Microsoft expected win on appeal to prevent the breakup.
