[711] After Judge Jackson ruled that Microsoft abused monopoly powers, Justice examined possible remedies to stop the company's monopolistic practices. [711] Options included even breaking up the company. [711] Justice and 17 states filed a brief asking for the breakup of Microsoft into two companies. [711] Microsoft countered with a proposed list of narrow restrictions on its behavior. [711] Microsoft cited a Justice document from an earlier case arguing that breaking up the company would be dangerous to the economy and against the public interest. [711] They argued that Judge Jackson relied on outdated antitrust concepts that not applicable to the fast-paced computer business.
