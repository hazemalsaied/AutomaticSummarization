[716] UNESCO's World Heritage Bureau gave the Australian government until April 15, 1999 to provide a comprehensive rebuttal to its report declaring Kakadu National Park endangered by the Jabiluka uranium mine. [716] Meanwhile, the government allowed work to continue, despite UNESCO's recommendation it be halted. [716] Australia's rebuttal cited errors of science, law and logic, and declared UNESCO's findings inconsistent with the fact that another nearby mine has operated for 20 years without adverse impact. [716] Critics of the Jabiluka mine include the Greens and local Mirrar Aboriginal people. [716] Two aboriginal protestors won the 1999 Goldman Environmental Prize for having delayed the mine project.
