[703] By mid-November 1998 France, Finland, Belgium, and Spain had begun to produce euro coins and Germany was "in an advanced stage" of experiments to strike them. [703] On the foreign scene in late 1998, by 21 November Indian banks were preparing for transactions in the euro and although Romania at that time was anticipating problems with the arrival of the euro, by mid-December its banking system was ready for the new currency. [703] On 31 December Bulgaria announced that its foreign currency reserves would be backed up by the euro instead of the German marks to which it had formerly been tied.
