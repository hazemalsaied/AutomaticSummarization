[711] In April 2000, Judge Jackson ruled that Microsoft violated the Sherman antitrust act. [711] He wrote that the company maintained its monopoly power by anticompetitive means, attempted to monopolize the web browser market and unlawfully tied its web browser to the operating system. [711] The Justice Department and 17 state attorneys general proposed breaking Microsoft up into two companies, one holding the operating system and the other everything else. [711] Judge Jackson agreed with the proposal. [711] Bill Gates announced that his company will appeal the verdict which was characterized as an anti-capitalist attack that would be harmful to Microsoft and the economy.
