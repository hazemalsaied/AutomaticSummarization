[D0716_D-F] After a decade of opposing new uranium mines, the Australian government approved construction of the Jabiluka mine in the Kakadu National Park, which is on the World Heritage List. [D0716_D-F] Environmentalists and opposition parties condemned the decision because of Kakadu's environmental and cultural value. [D0716_D-F] Aboriginal landowners threatened to block mine development. [D0716_D-F] Owners of the Jabiluka lease opposed the mine, but they are bound by a 1983 contract. [D0716_D-F] Protestors campaigned against the mine development and 350 protestors were arrested. [D0716_D-F] A week after mine construction began UNESCO's World Heritage Bureau began an investigation of claims that the area was under threat.
