[0]In October 1998, Matthew Shepard, a gay, 21-year old University of Wyoming college student was savagely beaten and tied to a fence in near-freezing temperatures. [1]He died five days later. [2]Matthew was lured out of a bar and attacked by two high-school dropouts, Aaron McKinney, 22 and Russell Henderson, 21. [3]McKinney was quoted as saying "It's Gay Awareness Week," as he beat Shepard. [4]The killing galvanized gays and lesbians nationwide. [5]Over 5,000 marched in Manhattan; candlelight vigils and campus rallies took place across the country. [6]Russel Henderson pleaded guilty and was sentenced to two consecutive life terms.
