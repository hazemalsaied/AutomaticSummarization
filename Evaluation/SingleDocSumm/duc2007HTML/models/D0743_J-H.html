[0]Two nearly simultaneous earthquakes, measuring 7.4 on the Richter scale, struck in the Izmit region southeast of Istanbul early in the morning of 17 August 1999. [1]The quakes, followed by 27 aftershocks, did extensive damage. [2]Latest reports indicate extensive damage with over 10,000 deaths, 45,000 injured and over 10,000 trapped in damaged buildings and houses. [3]The shocks have damaged bridges, highways, power stations, telephone lines and also triggered a large fire at the Izmit Refinery. [4]Foreign rescue teams from around the world are assisting in providing aid and rescue operations. [5]The earthquake may account for $20 billion in economic loss.
