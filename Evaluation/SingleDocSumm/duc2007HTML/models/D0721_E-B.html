[0]On October 12, 1998 gay Wyoming University student Matthew Shepard, 21, died after being kidnapped, pistol-whipped and tied to a fence in near-freezing temperatures outside Laramie, Wyoming, by Russell Henderson, 21, and Aaron McKinney, 22. [1]Hundreds were arrested as 5,000 rallied in New York City on October 19 to protest. [2]At trial in March 1999, Henderson pleaded guilty to kidnap and murder for two consecutive life sentences, with no parole. [3]Convicted accomplice after-the-fact, Chasity Pasley, faced sentencing. [4]Accomplice after-the-fact, Kristen Price, faced trial. [5]Shepard's mother called for anti-bias crime legislation. [6]Anti-gay bias measures died in Wyoming and other mountain states.
