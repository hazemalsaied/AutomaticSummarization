[0]In 1996, Energy Resources Australia requested approval for the Jabiluka uranium mine on the edge of the Kakadu National Park in the Northern Territory. [1]ERA's second uranium mine in the Kakadu World Heritage Area would generate billions of US dollars in revenue and create 2,000 jobs. [2]Opposition parties, conservationists and Aboriginal land owners opposed the planned mine. [3]However, studies found no adverse impact from ERA's other mine in the area. [4]After a protracted legal battle and amid continuing protests, ERA received approval and began mine construction in June 1998. [5]Police reported about 350 arrests as protesters attempted to block construction.
