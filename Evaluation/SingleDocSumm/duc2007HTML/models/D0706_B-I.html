[0]Aung San won Burma's freedom from British rule in 1947 and was assassinated. [1]The military took over in 1962, then the State Law and Order Restoration Council (SLORC) in 1988, massacring thousands and renaming Burma "Myanmar". [2]The government continually harassed Aung San's daughter Suu Kyi who headed the opposition. [3]She wrote the essay "Freedom From Fear" and won the 1991 Nobel Peace Prize. [4]The National League for Democracy (NLD) party won 82 percent of the 1990 vote but the military annulled the election. [5]Suu Kyi set Aug 21, 1998 for convening the National Assembly. [6]The government made 900 political arrests.
