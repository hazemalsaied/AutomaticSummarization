[0]Matthew Shepard, a gay University of Wyoming student, died after being in a coma for five days. [1]He had been robbed, beaten, and left to die on a fence outside of Laramie, Wyoming. [2]Two suspects, Aaron McKinney and Russell Henderson, were ordered to stand trial on first-degree murder charges. [3]Henderson pleaded guilty and was sentenced to two life terms. [4]McKinney went to trial. [5]The murder galvanized national outrage over anti-homosexual violence. [6]There were candlelight vigils across the country and a march in New York. [7]Religious right leaders, while deploring violence towards gays, insisted they hated the sin, not the sinner.
